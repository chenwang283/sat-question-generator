# Distinctness: same skill, original content

The goal is questions that test exactly what an official College Board item tests while
being genuinely original — not paraphrases of any official item. This serves two ends at
once: real practice (not memorizable clones of released questions) and rights (College
Board's published items are their copyrighted material, so we don't reproduce or closely
adapt them).

## The principle

**Preserve the function. Replace the content.**

- *Function* — the underlying skill, the cognitive demand, the question's structure, the
  kind of trap each distractor sets, and the difficulty. These should match the official
  item; that match is what makes the practice valid. (They're defined per skill in the
  guideline file.)
- *Content* — the specific passage, topic, scenario, names, numbers, phrasings, and answer
  values. These are freshly invented for every item.

A good test: set your item beside the official one it's modeled on. An expert should be
able to say both "these test the same thing the same way" *and* "these are clearly two
different questions" — no shared sentences, no shared passage, no shared specific values
beyond what the skill inherently requires.

## How the reference items are used

The generator reads the uploaded official items (its reference PDF) as **structural
anchors only**. From them, take:

- the question's shape and the stem's typical phrasing for this skill;
- the *category* of each distractor's trap (e.g. "sign error", "true but not the main
  idea").

Never take: the passage or any sentence of it, the specific scenario, the specific
numbers, the names, or the wording of the answer choices. Do not translate or lightly
reword an official passage — write a new passage on a different topic. The reference items
are precisely what each new item must be visibly *different from*.

## Rules of thumb for generation

- New topic and scenario for every item; vary topics across a set so the ten don't all
  orbit one subject.
- On Math, change the numbers *and* the resulting answer, not just the surrounding story.
- Original passages (Reading & Writing) are your own writing about plausible subjects —
  not excerpts or adaptations of real published text.
- If the only way you can make an item "different" is by swapping a few words of an
  official one, you haven't written a new item — start over from the skill, not from their
  question.

## What the distinctness check fails

The distinctness rule in a skill's verification section fails an item if:

- its passage or scenario is a recognizable paraphrase of a reference item;
- it shares distinctive wording, structure-plus-content, or answer values with one;
- its passage appears adapted from a specific real published source rather than newly
  written.

When in doubt, the verifier treats closeness as a fail and sends the item to refine for a
fresh rebuild — cheap insurance for both quality and rights.
